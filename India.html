 Introduction
 India is a country located in South Asia. It got its freedom from the British rule on 15th August 1947 after years of ceaseless struggle. It is made up of 28 states and 6 union territories. It  is bounded by the Himalayas in the north , Bangladesh and the Bay of Bengal in the east, the Indian ocean on the south and Pakistan and the Arabian Sea on the west .
 History
 India has a rich culture and history which dates back to the Indus Valley civilization thousands of years ago. The invasion of the Aryans, the establishment of Rajput kingdoms ,the Muslim invasions, the resistance of the Hindu rulers, the British conquest, the Sepoy Mutiny ,the freedom from British rule are all important chapters in the history of India. Numerous empires have rose and fell in India but ,as such, it has had a stable government for most of its history. The major empires in India from the past to the present in chronological order are:
  • The Mauryan Empire
  • The Gupta Emoire
  • The Delhi Sultanate
  • The Mughal Empire
  • The British Empire
  • The present-day Indian Union

 Geography
India has unique landscape ranging from the highest peaks to the lowest plains. The highest peak in India is a matter in dispute. Although Mt. K2 (Mount Godwin Austin), with an altitude of 8611m  is undoubtedly higher than Mt.Kanchenjunga (8,578m), Mt.K2 lies in PoK (Pakistan occupied Kashmir) . Both India and Pakistan consider the area as part of their territory . India can be divided into 5 main geographical divisions:

     The Western Ghats
  • The Himalayas
  • The Indo-Gangetic Plain
  • The Thar desert
  • The Deccan Plateau
  • The Coastal Plains
The Himalayan mountain ranges are situated to the extreme North of the country. The mountains sretch across the states of Jammu & Kashmir ,Himachal Pradesh, the northern tip of West Bengal, Assam and Arunachal Pradesh.
The Indo-Gangetic Plain lies in North India ,to the south of the Himalayas. The plains are very fertile. This is because because big rivers like the Ganga, Yamuna, Kosi, Chambal etc, flow through them depositing huge amounts of fertile silt in the Gangetic Plains.
The Thar desert is a small desert on the North-western frontier of India with Pakistan.It is confined to the state of Rajasthan. It les to the West of the Gangetic Plains. The reason it is a desert is because it is on the leeward side of the Aravalli mountains. Thus even though Mumbai ,which is just a few hundred kms away from the desert is battered by cyclones during the South-west monsoon, the Thar desert remains dry. The rain-bearing winds cannot pass the Aravallis.

 Religion
India is a secular country with total freedom of worship. The land is the birth place of various world like Hinduism, Buddhism, Sikhism, Jainism and also several smaller tribal religions. India is generally tolerant to foreign influence and thus there are also a large number of Muslims, Christians and Jews. However violent religious riots do break out from time to time. India has been ruled by the Buddhist Mauryas, the Hindu Guptas, the Muslim Mughals and the Christian British. This has made India a multi-cultural state or a "Mosaic of cultures" as described by India's first Prime Minster ,Jawaharlal Nehru.
Politics
Indian Politics is a very complex field as it consists of numerous regional political parties and very few national - level parties .The major national-level parties are the Indian National Congress(Congress), the right-wing Bharathiya Janata Party(B.J.P.) and the parties of the Left Front. Due to the large number of parties in India, it is virtually impossible for a single political party to form a government by means of simple majority in the Union Parliament of India without the support of of other regional political entities. For this reason, politics in India is mainly based on coalition system. As of 21st February ,2009 the Major political alliances in India are:
  • The United Progressive Alliance (led by the Congress) which is currently in power
  • The National Democratic Alliance (led by the B.J.P.) which is the main opposition and
  • The Third Front (led by the Left Front)
